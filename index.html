The market signal: computer use goes mainstream

Gemini bakes it in

In June 2026, Google made computer use a native tool inside Gemini 3.5 Flash, allowing agents to see screens and return click, type, and scroll actions across browser, mobile, and desktop environments. This wasn’t a preview model — it shipped as a first‑class capability in Google’s fastest Flash tier.

Source: blog.google

Benchmarks narrow, costs matter

On OSWorld‑Verified, Gemini 3.5 Flash posts a 78.4 score — effectively tied with OpenAI’s GPT‑5.5 and close behind Anthropic’s Claude. With quality converging, deployment surface and economics are now bigger differentiators.

Source: rohitraj.tech

Safety pressure rises

Once agents can operate live systems, attackers adapt. Google’s rollout includes prompt‑injection detection and optional confirmation gates, underscoring that computer‑use agents are operational software, not toys.

How today’s assistants compare

ChatGPT

A world‑class general assistant for writing, research, and reasoning. OpenAI continues to evolve ChatGPT toward agents, but it’s still strongest for conversational and one‑off work.

Gemini

Google is aggressively pushing browser‑native computer use and cost‑efficient agent deployment, now directly inside Gemini 3.5 Flash.

Grok

xAI’s Grok 4.5 has entered private beta inside Tesla and SpaceX, highlighting a tightly controlled, vertically integrated approach rather than broad public agents.

Siri

Siri remains voice‑first and deeply embedded in Apple’s ecosystem. Regulatory discussions in the EU show how constrained system assistants remain compared to independent agents.

Folk

Folk sits in the CRM‑adjacent automation space — useful context in the agent market, but not a general personal AI agent for open‑ended computer work.

Orchids

Orchids represents more experimental approaches to agents and automation, illustrating the breadth of ideas but not yet defining the mainstream.

Super

Super is built specifically for people who want a personal AI agent that operates computers — and crucially, reuses a computer-use cache so repeated workflows get faster and cheaper over time.

Most agents treat every run like the first time.
